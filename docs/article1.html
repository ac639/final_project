What is the WWW and what is it used for?

'WWW' stands for the World Wide Web, a term coined by Tim-Berners Lee.

Photo of Tim-Berners Lee


You may already know that the internet is just a network of computers and other networks. Tim-Berners Lee proposed the idea for the WWW to link these networks and computers together to host a collection of information that is accessible via URLs (Uniform Resource Locators) and hypertext.


Visual representation of the Web and how it is organized.

Today, information is shared on the world wide web through websites that act as containers for URLs, hypertext, images and videos. Modern websites are built using various markup and programming languages and frameworks. We will be looking at these in the next page.
